Everything you need to know about TaskAt

Getting Started

What is TaskAt?

TaskAt is an email-first client request and approval system. You forward a client email to TaskAt, it parses the thread and turns it into a trackable request. TaskAt supports 8 request types: Upload (files/documents), Approve (sign-offs), Answer (questions), Confirm (yes/no), Schedule (meeting times), Review (feedback), Checklist (multi-item tasks), and Rating (satisfaction). Your client receives an email with a clear action link, completes the task, and you see the result in your dashboard. No portals. No passwords for clients.

How does TaskAt work?

Forward: Forward any client email to TaskAt (e.g., upload@yourdomain.taskat.ai).
Parse: TaskAt reads the thread, extracts context, and drafts a request.
Review: You see the draft, edit if needed, and approve.
Send: Client gets an email with a clear action (upload file, approve, answer question).
Track: Watch progress in your dashboard. Auto-nudges keep things moving.

Do my clients need to create an account?

No. Clients receive requests via email and complete them without logging in. They click a secure link, take the action (upload, approve, answer), and they're done. Optional: clients can access a portal to see all their requests in one place, but it's not required.

What's the best way to start my pilot?

Start with one real thread. Forward a client email you've been chasing. See TaskAt create the request, watch the client complete it, and experience the difference. Most teams expand from there naturally.

AI Operator - Assist+ (Beta)

What is AI Operator - Assist+?

Assist+ is our optional AI layer. When enabled, it can auto-send low-risk requests (Upload + Answer) without waiting for your review-when it's confident the request is clear and safe. Approval requests (sign-offs, decisions) always require your review. You stay in control.

What safety controls does Assist+ have?

Type-based rules: Upload + Answer can auto-send. Approvals never auto-send.
Confidence threshold: Default 0.85 — only sends when AI is highly confident.
Trust seeding: Requires at least one manual request to a contact before auto-send.
Business hours: Respects configurable send windows.
Daily caps & cooldowns: Prevents client email fatigue.
Blocked domains/contacts: Exclude specific recipients.
Audit log: Every decision logged in automation_runs table with reasoning.
Fallback: If unsure, creates draft (DRAFT_ONLY) for your review.

Can I disable Assist+ entirely?

Yes. Assist+ is opt-in and can be disabled at any time. Without it, TaskAt works in draft-first mode: every request waits for your review before sending.

Email Threading & Replies

How does email threading work?

When you forward an email with a thread history, TaskAt reads and stores the full conversation. Clients see the original context in their request. When they reply, TaskAt routes responses to the correct request-even if the subject line changes or they reply from a different device.

How does reply matching work?

TaskAt uses multiple signals: Message-ID headers, In-Reply-To references, subject line patterns, sender address, and timing. Even if a client replies from a different email app or changes the subject, we match it to the right request with high accuracy.

What happens when a client replies?

Replies are automatically classified: Is this a completion? A question? An objection? TaskAt updates the request status, notifies you if action is needed, and can auto-respond to simple questions or escalate complex ones.

Request Types

What types of requests can TaskAt handle?

Upload: Request files, assets, or documents (tax docs, contracts, logos).
Approve: Get sign-off on deliverables, designs, or decisions.
Answer: Ask questions that need a response.
Confirm: Simple yes/no acknowledgements.
Schedule: Request meeting times or availability.
Review: Collect feedback on documents or deliverables.
Checklist: Multi-item task lists (great for onboarding).
Rating: Star ratings for satisfaction surveys.

What are the request status states?

Requests flow through these canonical statuses:
draft — Created but not sent yet
waiting — Sent, waiting on client response
in_review — Client responded, needs your review
completed — Resolved/finished
paused — Temporarily paused (no nudges sent)
cancelled — Intentionally stopped

How do uploads work?

Clients click a secure upload link, drag-and-drop or select files, and submit. Files are stored securely and appear in your dashboard immediately. No email attachment size limits. Supports all common file types.

How do approvals work?

Clients see the deliverable (image, PDF, or link), then click Approve or Request Changes. If they request changes, they can add notes. You see the decision instantly and can act on feedback.

What's the difference between Confirm and Approve?

Confirm: Simple yes/no acknowledgement—no changes possible. Use for receipts, policy acknowledgements, or "got it" confirmations.
Approve: Decision with options—approve or request changes with notes. Use for deliverables, budgets, or anything requiring feedback.

How do Schedule requests work?

You send a request asking for availability. The client sees a simple interface to select from time slots you've offered or propose alternatives. Once they respond, you're notified and can confirm the meeting.

How do Checklist requests work?

You create a list of items the client needs to complete. They see each item with a checkbox and can mark them done one by one. You see real-time progress and get notified when everything is complete. Great for onboarding or pre-launch requirements.

How do Rating requests work?

Clients see a star rating (1-5) with optional comment field. Simple and quick to complete. Use for project satisfaction, monthly check-ins, or NPS-style feedback. Results appear in your dashboard for tracking trends.

AI & Automation

What AI agents power TaskAt?

TaskAt uses four Claude-powered AI agents:
1. Thread Extraction Agent: Analyzes forwarded emails and identifies actionable requests with confidence scores.
2. Autopilot Engine (Assist+): Decides whether to auto-send, create a draft, or block based on policy rules.
3. Reply Classification Agent: Interprets client replies as approved, rejected, or ambiguous.
4. Nudge Generation Agent: Creates contextual follow-up emails with appropriate tone escalation.

What happens when AI confidence is low?

When the Thread Extraction Agent's confidence score falls below your threshold (default 0.85), the request is created as a draft (DRAFT_ONLY) for your review. You can edit, adjust the request type, or discard it entirely. Low confidence never results in an auto-send.

Can I see why the AI made a decision?

Yes. Every AI decision is logged in the ai_decision_logs table with full input/output data. For Assist+ automation, each run is logged in automation_runs with the specific reason (AUTO_SEND, DRAFT_ONLY, or BLOCKED) and which policy rule triggered it.

Can I tell if a request was created by AI or a person?

Yes. Every request tracks attribution:
created_by: USER — Created manually by an agency person
created_by: AI — Extracted by the Thread Extraction Agent
send_mode: MANUAL — Person clicked send
send_mode: AUTOPILOT — Assist+ auto-sent the request
This helps with auditing and understanding your workflow patterns.

Nudges & Follow-ups

Can I pause or stop nudges for a client?

Yes. You can pause nudges on any request with one click. Paused requests stay visible but won't send any follow-ups until you resume. You can also set per-client preferences to limit frequency or disable auto-nudges entirely.

How often does TaskAt send follow-ups?

Default schedule: first nudge after 3 days, then 5 days, then 7 days. You can customize this per request type or per client. Daily caps prevent any client from receiving more than one nudge per day.

Can clients unsubscribe from nudges?

Yes. Every nudge email includes a preference link. Clients can reduce frequency or pause reminders for specific requests. You're notified when clients adjust preferences so you can follow up manually if needed.

Email & Inbox Support

What email inboxes does TaskAt support?

TaskAt works with any email provider that supports forwarding: Gmail, Google Workspace, Outlook, Microsoft 365, Apple Mail, Yahoo, and more. If you can forward an email, you can use TaskAt. No integrations or plugins required.

Will TaskAt emails land in spam?

We optimize for deliverability: proper SPF/DKIM/DMARC authentication, dedicated sending IPs with clean reputation, automatic bounce handling, and content that avoids spam triggers. Custom domain setup further improves inbox placement. Most clients see 98%+ inbox rates.

Do I need to install anything?

No. TaskAt is 100% web-based. Forward emails from your existing inbox, manage requests in your browser. No browser extensions, desktop apps, or plugins required.

Security & Privacy

Is TaskAt secure?

Yes. Defense-in-depth security: All data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Files are virus-scanned with ClamAV before storage. Portal tokens are bcrypt-hashed (never stored plaintext). Rate limiting: 100 req/15min (public), 20 req/hour (tokens). MIME validation on uploads. IP hashing for GDPR compliance. We don't sell or share your data. SOC 2 compliance in progress.

What happens if an email bounces or generates a complaint?

TaskAt automatically handles bounces and complaints. Bounced addresses are flagged, and we pause sending to problematic addresses to protect your sender reputation. You're notified so you can update contact info.

Can I use my own domain for sending emails?

Yes. You can set up custom domain sending (e.g., requests@youragency.com) with proper SPF/DKIM authentication. This improves deliverability and brand recognition.

Pricing & Pilot

What's included in the 14-day free pilot?

Full access to all features with pilot limits: 50 requests sent, 10 active clients, 100 AI analyses, and 100MB storage. We help you set up, migrate existing threads if needed, and check in weekly. At the end, you choose to upgrade or stay in view-only mode (no sending, but you keep access to everything).

What is an active client?

A client becomes "active" when you send them at least one request. Clients with no open requests in the past 30 days don't count toward your limit.

What counts as an outbound email?

Outbound emails are messages TaskAt sends to your clients: requests, follow-ups, nudges, and clarifications. Forwarded emails from you and client replies are free and unlimited.

What happens after my pilot ends?

After 14 days (or when limits are reached), sending pauses but you keep full access to view your dashboard, client history, and all uploaded files. Upgrade anytime to resume sending.

Can I keep using TaskAt without upgrading?

Yes-view-only mode. You can see everything, download files, and review history. You just can't send new requests or follow-ups until you upgrade.
